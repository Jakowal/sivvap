## History
### Founding
Tandia is the first nation to rise after [[The Time of Chaos#Tandia is Founded|1446 T.B.C]]. Having forged alliances during their survival, the founding dukes decided to band together in order to strengthen their position in the world.
### Early History
By being such an early institution of order, Tandia was instrumental in the creation of [[The Guilds|The Guilds]]. By estalishing itself as someone who would pay handsomely for fine craftmanship, they attracted skilled labour in droves. This then laid the groundwork for those workers joining forces to create the exclusive guilds. The Duchies didn't much like this development, as it made the labour more expensive than when they could deal with each worker individually, something that has created a rift between the guilds and duchies that still remains.

However, the workers (and later, guilds) helped build up the major cities in each duchy at record speed. And over the next decade, Tandia became a bastion of safety and progress. A safe place for everyone to settle down. So long as they didn't have any plans of challenging the social order.
### Neighbours
As the world entered the [[The Present#Important Events|Years of The Book]], more nations started declaring themselves. First [[Drazkyania]] in [[The Present#Drazkyania Drazkyania is Founded|4 Y.B]] and soon after [[Akamar]], [[Eruvia]], [[Vakandar]] and [[Cavaris]] over the next decade. No land the duchies had staked a claim to was being taken by this. Nonetheless, the ducal families felt their interests were threatened and at the next council, they decided to respond.

With Eruvia being slow to develop any military presence of their own, the dukes decided it was an easy target. After a series of quick skirmishes, the overwhelming might of the Tandian army took over the country and it was turned into a puppet state.

But other countries were not happy with this development. Vakandar and Cavaris, both sharing larger borders with the conquerors, decided they would not wait until it was their turn. Already [[The Present#Vakandar and Cavaris invade Tandia|the next year]], they responded to the aggression by invading Tandia. The war lasted four bloody years and in the end, Tandia was victorious, claiming its foes as puppet states as well. Thus, Tandia cemented itself as the greatest military power in the world.
### The Khazantin Conversion
Having expanded their reach so much, the dukes found it difficult to maintain control over their population. Fortunately for them, [[The Book of Khazanti|The Book of Khazanti]] had started to become a widespread religion around the world. Especially in their puppet states its word was becoming a common belief. The ducal families plotted that if they turned their nine duchies into divinely appointed seats of power, they could ride this religious wave and regain control of their puppets.

With manipulation, large sums of money for religious infrastructure, and revisions to the official history, they started the process of converting their country. Naturally, there are few official records of this dark period in the country's history. However, personal records and oral histories show that it clearly wasn't a voluntary conversion amongst the Tandians at first. But after a generation or two, the deed was done.

Their plot worked and their peoples of Eruvia, Vakandar and Cavaris gradually ceased resisting their integration in the Tandian empire. It took longer for their own people to fully accept their new religion, but towards the end of the 30's Tandia was a peaceful Khazantin nation.
### The Ducal Reign
Now undisputably the grandest nation of the known world, Tandia enjoyed a long period of peace. Over the next century, the duchies fully focused its energies inwards. While they were unenthused with the power of [[The Guilds|The Guilds,]] the dukes employed their services in building the foundations of a strong nation. Proper roads and bridges were erected between cities and walls were created to keep the wilds out. Down under the ground, systems of lifts and pulleys were created to ease transport down into the newly-built docks and travel by sea became more and more affordable.

Beyond just building up the already world leading infrastructure, the dukes set their armies to focus on the safety of their nation. Though they kept token forces to defend borders, a majority of their armies were diverted inwards. The effects of this were immediate. Roads became safer and attacks on villages rarer. Trade between the duchies flourished and the people were happy, wealthy and secure.
### Fall From Grace
There is some debate as to when the Tandian empire started to fall apart, but the most commonly agreed upon timeframe is the middle of the second century Y.B. At this point, the dukes were running out of things to improve in their nation. This meant the economy that so far had been flourishing, started stagnating. With this, displeasure grew and eventually, the vassal states began debating whether or not they should remain within the empire.

In an attempt to both stimulate the economy again, and dissuade any notion that their vassals should try freeing themselves, Tandia invaded [[Yntol]] in [[The Present#The Tandian Troubles|162 Y.B.]] The resulting war was short, only lasting a couple of months, but it devastated the Tandian forces. Anyone who previously thought Tandia might have been a powerful military nation realized this was no longer the case. During the following economic crisis, [[Eruvia]] saw its chance to quickly withdraw from the empire. The dukes were still caught off-guard by their defeat and were unable to mount any sort of resistance to it. 

Reacting to this, both [[Vakandar]] and [[Cavaris]] decided it was time for them to withdraw from the empire as well. However, the dukes had foreseen this event and managed to marshal what forces they had left to stop it. Though they were unsuccessful, the conflict was costly for both armies. In the end, both nations were liberated and the empire was no more.
### What Remains
The following half-century was harsh on the remaining Tandian citizens. The ducal families turned on each-other, unable to decide whose fault the recent turn of events was. Any infrastructure outside of the main cities fell into disrepair as there were neither people, nor the funds to maintain it. Monsters once again spread around the nation and travel became too hazardous and expensive for most. Because this caused trade to grind to a halt,  [[The Guilds|guild]] operations outside of the main cities ceased entirely and skileld labour became even harder to come by.

As time passed, the dukes became more and more removed from their people, instead focusing on thwarting the other families. Other countries started hiring mercenary bands to prod at Tandia's defences, making life near the borders harsh, and relations between the outer and inner duchies even more strained. But still, the dukes managed to maintain control of the country. Towards the third century, there were rebellions forming, but they were crushed with such force that none since have tried to openly oppose the ducal families.

## Society
*Subsections: [[Tandian Fashion|Fashion]], [[Naming Conventions in Tandia|Naming Conventions]]*
### A Fractured Nation
The nation is composed of several smaller duchies, each led by their own duke. There is no central government that rules over these dukes. Rather, they gather thrice a year for a council in which they decide on the few matters that affect them all. These matters can be anything from inter-ducal disputes to how many they need to draft for the army in order to keep their borders safe.

The threshold for what the council actually makes a ruling on, is quite high. Thus, each duchy has ended up with its own standing military force, both for use against other duchies and aggressive nations. This is a symptom of how the ducal families historically always mistrusted each other, though they for a long time had common goals. Now that those goals have fallen down the priority list, infighting has become the norm, sometimes even escalating to small skirmishes with their armies.

Because of this infighting and lack of general unity, Tandia is often regarded as rather fragile by other nations and even its own people at times. Despite their rich history and vast resources, the dukes rarely band together unless something is dangerous enough to threaten them all. This attitude has led to their neighbors sun- and frostwwards often testing the borders with small raids. Never anything more than mercenary bands, but enough that the border dukes grow even more angry with their inland counterparts that they dont get the support they feel they need. Meanwhile, the inner duchies feel they already give too much support in safeguarding land that isn't even theirs.
### Education
Though the country has seen little social progress over the past half-century, there are many values that remain from the [[Tandia#The Ducal Reign|golden age]] of the empire. Among these are a focus on education. Schools around the country are still well supplied with willing teachers, and the population has a high general level of education. Originally, the dukes started this as a countermeasure to [[The Guilds#Personal Responsibility|The Guilds]], given their historical distaste for them. While the schools haven't been able to stop guild dependency, they have managed to make the population of Tandia *less* reliant on guild artisans than many other countries. Of course mages are no more common among the populace than anywhere else, meaning [[The Guilds#Mages Guild|The Mages Guild]] still holds an important role.
